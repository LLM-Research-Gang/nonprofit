# Parsa TL;DR

This memo summarizes, in practical and legal terms, what it means for `Parsa Rahimi` to serve as a `Director` only, and not as an officer, of `Economics of Generative AI Research Lab`, a California nonprofit public benefit corporation intended to qualify under Section `501(c)(3)` of the Internal Revenue Code.

## 1. Current role structure

Under the current structure:

- Parsa would serve as a `Director`;
- Parsa would not serve as `Treasurer`, `Secretary`, `President`, or any other officer role; and
- the officer responsibilities associated with `Secretary` and `Treasurer` would instead sit with `Jake Cosgrove`.

That distinction matters because director service carries governance obligations, while officer service carries additional executive and administrative responsibility.

## 2. What director service implies

If Parsa accepts appointment as a `Director`, he would be expected to act:

- in good faith;
- in a manner he reasonably believes to be in the best interests of the corporation;
- with such care, including reasonable inquiry, as an ordinarily prudent person in a like position would use under similar circumstances; and
- consistently with the corporation's Articles, Bylaws, board resolutions, conflict rules, and applicable California and federal law.

The role is fiduciary in nature. It is not merely symbolic if he is participating in governance.

## 3. Core director responsibilities

As a `Director`, Parsa would be expected to:

- participate in board decisions with reasonable diligence;
- review and approve major organizational actions;
- help preserve the corporation's charitable, educational, and scientific purposes;
- monitor compliance with nonprofit, tax-exemption, and corporate-governance requirements;
- disclose actual or potential conflicts of interest promptly; and
- avoid using the organization for private benefit, self-dealing, or non-exempt purposes.

As a practical matter, this means reading the governing documents, voting carefully, asking questions where needed, and keeping attention on mission, compliance, and financial integrity.

## 4. What he is not taking on

Because Parsa would not be an officer, he would not, by virtue of title alone, be the person primarily responsible for:

- maintaining corporate minutes and records;
- handling banking authority or operational signatures as an officer;
- overseeing routine bookkeeping or financial administration as Treasurer; or
- carrying out officer-level filing and administrative functions assigned by the board.

Those responsibilities would instead attach to the designated officers, subject to any separate board resolutions or delegated tasks.

## 5. Lawyer note

The current practical guidance received from counsel is:

`Not an issue, nope, as long as Parsa is not doing any work.`

Operationally, that suggests the concern is reduced if Parsa is serving in a nominal governance capacity and is not separately performing compensated or ongoing operational work for the organization. This is a practical reading of the note, not a substitute for fuller legal advice on the scope of his board participation.

## 6. Bottom line

If Parsa accepts, he should understand that director service still carries fiduciary and governance consequences, but he would not be assuming the additional legal and operational responsibilities associated with an officer post. On the current structure, his role is board governance, not officer administration.

This memo is a practical internal summary and is not a substitute for advice from California nonprofit counsel or a qualified tax-exempt organizations attorney.
