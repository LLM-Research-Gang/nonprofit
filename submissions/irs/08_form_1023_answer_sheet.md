## Part IV. Your Activities

### 1. Describe completely and in detail your past, present, and planned activities

Use this narrative:

`Economics of Generative AI Research Lab is organized to conduct and disseminate independent educational and scientific research on artificial intelligence, large language models, AI agents, and their economic effects. Its activities are designed to advance public understanding of how AI systems affect decision-making, economic behavior, institutions, and human interaction with emerging technologies.`

`The organization expects to carry out four main activity areas. First, it will conduct research on AI agents as objects of study, including experiments and analysis concerning preferences, decision-making patterns, biases, and differences across models, prompting conditions, and deployment settings. This work will be conducted primarily by directors, collaborating academic colleagues, and student researchers. It will be conducted primarily online and through standard research collaboration workflows. The organization currently expects this activity to account for about 35 percent of its time and resources. It will be funded through donations and grants, and its expenses will consist mainly of research costs, experiment costs, software and compute tools, and publication costs. This activity furthers the organization's exempt educational and scientific purposes by producing independent research and public scholarship on economically significant AI systems.`

`Second, the organization will conduct research on agent-to-agent interaction and the emerging agent economy. This includes conceptual and empirical work on negotiation, coordination, competition, market-like interaction, and other structured multi-agent settings. This work will be conducted primarily by directors and academic collaborators, mainly online and through collaborative research settings. The organization currently expects this activity to account for about 30 percent of its time and resources. It will be funded through donations and grants, and the related expenses will include experiment costs, software and compute tools, travel, and publication costs. This activity furthers exempt purposes by generating and disseminating educational and scientific knowledge about how AI agents interact in economically relevant environments.`

`Third, the organization will conduct research on human interaction with AI systems, including how humans perceive, trust, evaluate, and respond to AI agents and AI-generated outputs. This may include pilot work, controlled studies, and human-subject experiments where appropriate. This work will be conducted primarily by directors and student researchers, mainly online and in research settings. The organization currently expects this activity to account for about 20 percent of its time and resources. It will be funded through donations and grants, and the related expenses will include human experiment costs, software tools, and publication costs. This activity furthers exempt purposes by producing public-interest educational and scientific research about how people interact with emerging technologies.`

`Fourth, the organization will develop and share methodologies, benchmarks, and reproducibility-oriented research practices for studying AI systems. This activity will overlap with the organization's substantive research areas and is expected to account for about 15 percent of its time and resources. It will be conducted primarily by directors and collaborators through research and publication workflows. Funding will come from donations and grants, and expenses will include software tools, publication costs, and related research support. This activity furthers exempt purposes by improving research quality, comparability, and public understanding in a rapidly developing field.`

`The organization intends to publish papers, working papers, public research summaries, methodological materials, and related educational outputs. It may also present work through workshops, seminars, lectures, and collaborations. It is not organized to operate a consulting business, product business, startup, or paid private-client research firm. It does not plan to engage in political campaign activity, and it does not expect lobbying to be a substantial part of its activities. Any intellectual property created, such as papers, educational materials, datasets, or benchmarks, will be used to further the organization's exempt purposes through publication, dissemination, and related educational use. The organization does not currently plan to compensate directors or officers during its initial period of operation.`
